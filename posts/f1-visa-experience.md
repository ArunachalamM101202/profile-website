## What is this F1 Student Visa?

The F1 Student Visa is a non-immigrant visa provided to international students to pursue their education in the United States at an accredited institution. It is a **temporary visa** and does not allow you to live there permanently. I have provided the official US government link that explains the Student Visa in detail.

Student Visa Information Link: [https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/study/student-visa.html](https://travel.state.gov/content/travel/en/us-visas/study/student-visa.html)

## Visa Application Process

The Visa Application process consists of the following steps:

1.  DS-160 Process
2.  SEVIS Fee Payment
3.  Visa Appointment Scheduling

### DS-160 Process

1.  First, create a DS-160 application at https://ceac.state.gov/genniv/ and note down the DS160 Application ID.
2.  Fill out the DS-160 carefully as all the details you enter here will be used by the Consulate for issuing your visa.
3.  The website keeps refreshing frequently due to security reasons, so ensure you save the application each time you complete a section
4.  Ask your parents/reliable friends/mentors/seniors to review your DS-160 application to check for errors. It's always good to ask another person to review your application for mistakes as they will view it from a new perspective
5.  After thorough checking, submit your DS-160 Application. **Note: There will be no payment during this process**
6.  After submission, you will receive the **DS-160 confirmation page** pdf which is **important for your Visa Interview** so ensure you save this receipt.

### SEVIS (Student and Exchange Visitor Information System) Fee Payment

> _Note: (This process can be done any time, but must be completed_ **_at least 3 days_** **_before your Biometric Slot_**_)_

1.  This process is mandated by the US Congress to support the overall SEVIS system
2.  SEVIS ID is generated by the first University you request your i20. The SEVIS ID will be mentioned at the top left of your i20 in bold
3.  If you have requested i20 from multiple universities, your SEVIS ID will remain the same.
4.  Visit [https://www.fmjfee.com/i901fee/index.html](https://www.fmjfee.com/i901fee/index.html) and fill in your necessary details such as name, SEVIS ID, etc
5.  In the next screen, you will be prompted to pay 350$ (around 30,000 INR). Use a credit card for this payment
6.  After payment is successful, you will receive a receipt known as the **SEVIS Receipt**. This is very **important for your Visa Interview**, so ensure you save this receipt

### Visa Appointment Scheduling

1.  This is one of the most important steps, so ensure you do this step carefully
2.  Visit [https://www.usvisascheduling.com/en-US/](https://www.usvisascheduling.com/en-US/) and create an account. Remember the password and answers to all the 3 security questions
3.  Log in to your account and start filling in the basic details such as name, address, etc
4.  In the subsequent screens you will be asked to enter your DS-160 Application ID and SEVIS ID, ensure you enter them correctly!!
5.  In another section you will be asked about the mode of delivery of your passport, Choose Delivery (costs around 850 INR) if you need it quickly or you are located very far from your pick-up location, else choose pickup.
6.  Fill in the other details accordingly, and on the last screen, you will be prompted to make a payment of 185$ (around 15000 INR).
7.  Complete the payment (payment can be made using UPI or credit card) and wait for 2 hours for the receipt to be generated
8.  After 2 hours, you will be shown a "Submit" button which submits the payment receipt automatically
9.  Now henceforth you will enter the slot booking screen once you log in.

### How to book a Visa Slot?

1.  Login to [https://www.usvisascheduling.com/en-US/](https://www.usvisascheduling.com/en-US/)
2.  Select your preferred consulate and then select your preferred date
3.  For each date, you will be shown the number of slots available per hour such as (8:30 am — 20 slots, 9:00 am — 20 slots etc). Try to select the time that has more slots to increase your chances of confirming the particular slot.
4.  Once you click next, you have to do steps 2 and 3 again. The first time you select your Biometric/OFC and the second time you select Consular Interview.
5.  Note: You have to book both slots at the same time to complete the scheduling process and cannot do one slot and exit.
6.  Once you book both slots and click submit, you will receive the **Visa Appointment Confirmation** pdf which is **important for your Biometric and Consular Interview**.

### Some important points to keep in mind while booking your visa slot

1.  The website is very slow and crashes often, so don't be worried if it isn't loading for you
2.  Join various telegram groups to get quick updates for Visa slot openings.
3.  If in the case while you login and see **_"Waiting room"_**_,_ kindly **don't refresh the page** as it will allow you a few minutes, and refreshing will move your request to the end
4.  Have multiple tabs in multiple browsers opened if you see **_"Waiting room"_** during peak slot booking hours. My suggestion is to use Brave Browser or Edge as it is very fast on Mac OS and Windows. Use Firefox if you use Linux.
5.  There will be a lot of people booking their slots, so if you do not get a slot in the first few tries, do not lose hope as there are ample numbers of slots that will be opened from May mid to July end exclusively for students.
6.  You can choose different consulates for Biometric and Interview

> **_Note: This is purely a personal opinion:_** _I have noticed Mumbai and Chennai Consulates to have very less rejections compared to Delhi, Kolkata, and Hyderabad. So try for Mumbai and Chennai first if you are located nearby._

## Biometric Process

This is the first step of your F1 Visa process where you will visit the biometric center mentioned in your **Visa Appointment Confirmation**.

Visit the biometric center **_at least 30 minutes_** before your slot and follow the instructions provided at the center. I had selected Chennai for Biometric as it is very nearby and went to my center around 9:55 am and my slot was at 11:30 am. At Chennai Biometric Centre they allowed everyone irrespective of slots but this isn't the same scenario for other consulates as they might ask you to wait for a long time. So ensure you reach your biometric center around 30 minutes prior.

**Chennai Biometric Centre Address:**

No 82, Kodambakkam High Road, Nungambakkam, Chennai 600034

**Date:** June 3rd, 2024 (Monday)

**Slot Time:** 11:30 am

**Dress:** Blue Shirt and a Silver Formal Pant.

### Documents Required for Biometric:

1.  DS-160 Confirmation Page
2.  SEVIS Fee Receipt
3.  Passport (Old and New Passport)
4.  Visa Appointment Confirmation
5.  Signed I20
6.  Photo (US dimensions) (if possible)

**Try to have 2 copies of all the documents** mentioned above for safety reasons and have a transparent suitcase file like this as it will help you a lot while taking the necessary documents

### My Biometric Experience:

I reached the Chennai Biometric Centre at 9:55 am and joined the waiting queue outside the center. The officer allowed me inside even though my slot was at 11:30 am after checking my Visa Appointment Confirmation. Next, I was directed to the 2nd floor of the building where the Biometric Center is present and was asked to join a queue. There will be many volunteers to guide you through the queue and one of them asked me to keep the Passport, DS-160 confirmation page, and Visa Appointment Confirmation in my hand.

As the queue progressed, there was a standard checking where they would ask to keep your **mobile phones in switched-off mode.** Then as the queue progressed, I handed over my passport and DS-160 Confirmation page to an officer who verified the details and asked my name for confirmation. Then they placed a sticker on the back of my passport, and handed me back the documents.

Then as I continued the queue, the volunteers would divide the queue according to a Visa officer based on vacancy. The Biometric Visa Officers were Indians at the Chennai Biometric Centre. My Biometric Visa officer was sitting behind a glass wall and spoke through a mic. There will be a speaker near you and the audio will always be very clear. I first greeted my officer and then the officer asked for my Passport and DS-160 confirmation which I passed through the small hole in between the glass. The officer asked me to state my name and date of birth which I did and then she asked me to **place my left 4 fingers on a fingerprint scanner**. Next, she asked me to **place my right 4 fingers and my thumbs subsequently**. Then she asked me to sit straight and **pose for a photograph**.

> **_Tip: Don't wear a white shirt for the Biometric Process as the background is white and your photo won't look good. This is an important photo as it will be printed on your passport along with your F1 Visa._**

After the photograph was taken, the officer stamped my DS-160 confirmation page and returned my documents. Then I was directed towards the exit. The entire process took around 20 minutes as there were very few people at the Chennai Biometric Centre.

## Visa Interview Process

This is the next and final step of your F1 Visa process where you will visit the consulate office mentioned in your **Visa Appointment Confirmation.**

Visit the center at least 45 minutes prior to your slot and follow the instructions provided at the center. You can have your mobile phone switched off and it is usually better if you don't wear watches and other metal gadgets as the officers might ask you questions regarding it.

I had selected Chennai again for the Consular Interview as it was nearby. I reached the Consular Office at 8:15 am and my slot was at 9:30 am. Irrespective of the slot, the officers at Chennai allowed people inside. So if you have selected Chennai, you can go even an hour prior as well. Kindly note that Chennai has different addresses for Biometric and Interview processes.

220 Anna Salai, Opp.Gemini Flyover, Gemini Circle, Chennai 600006.

**Date:** June 6th, 2024 (Thursday)

**Slot Time:** 9:30 am

**Dress:** Blue Shirt and a Silver Formal Pant.

**Visa Documents**

3. Old and new passports

4. Visa Appointment Confirmation printout

5. Sevis Fees Confirmation printout

6. Photo ( US Dimensions )

**Education and Other Documents**

1.  10th and 12th Marksheet

2. Transcripts

3. Semester Mark sheets

4. Current Resume

5. Any useful curricular and extra certificates

6. Degree Certificate (if the degree is completed )

7. Course completion or professional certificate

8. Admit Letter copy

9. GRE and English proficiency

10. LOR (1 LOR which you had submitted would be enough)

11. SOP of the University you are planning to join

12. Intern and working documents if any and an ITR of yours if you are currently working

13. Lease Document which consists of the address of your location (if you have confirmed your stay in the US)

> You might be wondering why there are so many documents, the reason is that we never know which document the Visa Officer will ask and it is always safe to have all the documents ready to show them.

It might seem overwhelming to require so many documents along with photocopies, but remember that you will need these photocopies when you leave for the US, so you can use them then as well.

> **Note: Kindly have all the original documents along with a photocopy of each document. You must always show the original document to the Visa Officer.**

## My Visa Interview Process

I reached the Consular office at 8:15 am and joined the waiting queue. The queue consisted of people applying for tourist visas, L1 visas, and other visas as well so it was pretty crowded compared to the Biometric Centre. I was waiting in the queue outside the center and then an officer checked my Visa Appointment Confirmation and allowed me inside.

There was a routine checking for metal objects and I was requested to keep my mobile phone in switched off mode. People who wore watches were asked to remove them and keep them inside their files/pockets. Then I joined a waiting area and waited for 20 minutes after which I joined a waiting queue which was very long and volunteers checked my passport and DS-160 confirmation in-between. The queue was very long as I spent nearly an hour waiting. Finally, the volunteers directed the queue to the available Visa Officers. I was directed accordingly to Counter 17 and the following was the interaction with the officer:

### Important points to keep in mind during the Visa Interview

1.  People in front of you or nearby you might get rejected, so don't feel nervous seeing them and perform your interview poorly.
2.  Always greet your visa officer with a smile. First impression is very important.
3.  Try to dress formally and follow the basic etiquette throughout the process
4.  Listen to the visa officer's complete question before answering and be very crisp and clear while answering
5.  Have a positive mindset
6.  Remember to mention you will come back to India if they ask about your plans after graduation. If you mention you will stay in the US after graduation, they will reject your visa.
7.  The Visa officer will primarily check whether you are planning to stay in the US only for studies or planning to extend your stay. So ensure you answer accordingly.

### Sample Visa Interview Questions

Here I'll be providing some of the most popular Visa Interview questions along with the answers I had in mind for a few of them. However, I suggest you to refer various articles and websites to get to know lot of questions and answers.

Question: Full form of visa?

Answer: Visitors International Stay Admission

Question: What is SEVIS?

Answer: Student Exchange Visitor Information System, used to monitor the status of all nonimmigrant students

Question: Why did you choose the US?

Answer: My aim is to study Computer Science and the United States is the world leader in terms of research universities for Computer Science. Universities in the US offer more practical-based learning compared to other countries and faculties have vast experience in the field of CS.

Question: Why can't you continue your education in your home country?

Answer: The US has many top-level world-class institutions and getting a degree from such a top-level university in computer science is my goal. It will help boost my profile overall. Universities in the US offer more practical-based learning compared to other countries and faculties have vast experience in the field of CS.

Question: What are you planning to study?

Answer: Answer according to your course

Question: Have you been to the United States before?

Answer: Answer truthfully if you have visited before

Question: What courses and faculty have you enrolled in?

Answer: Explain accordingly

Question: Where is the university located?

Answer: Provide the University Address mentioned in the I20

Question: Where are you planning to stay?

Answer: Mention the address of the apartment you have booked and mention how close it is to the University in terms of miles. If you haven't booked yet, mention that you have almost finalized an accommodation.

Question: Why did you choose this university?

Answer: Read about the university and mention the highlights of the particular university in relevance to the course you are taking.

Question: Why this particular campus?

Answer: Answer accordingly

Question: How many different universities did you apply to?

Answer: Mention the number and provide all the university names you applied to if the visa officer asks further.

Question: How are you planning to finance your education?

Answer: Answer accordingly.

> **Note: Ensure you mention all the amount in USD and not in INR**

Question: Who is sponsoring you?

Answer: Answer accordingly

Question: What is the profession of your sponsor?

Answer: Explain their designation, and number of years they are in the field and mention annual income if possible (again in USD)

Question: Do you plan on working while you are studying in the US?

Answer: I'm planning to focus more on studying during my time at _____ and build my profile by working on projects. So as of now I have no plans on working on campus.

**Important Question: Do you plan to return to your home country after completing your studies? (or) Do you have a job or career in mind post-graduation?**

Answer: After graduation, using the skills and technology I learned at _____, I will come back to India and search for software development jobs preferably in Chennai. Also, receiving my Masters Degree from such a reputed university will boost my profile overall which will increase the chances of me securing a software development job in India.

Question: Do you have family, relatives, or friends in the US?

Answer: Answer accordingly
