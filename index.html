About Me

Hi, I’m a Computer Science graduate student at the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities, with a focus on AI, Machine Learning, Natural Language Processing, and Advanced Software Engineering. Beyond coursework, I work as a Graduate Research Assistant in the Epidemiology department, where I help model HPV-related cancer outcomes. I also serve as a Teaching Assistant at the Carlson School of Management, guiding students through hands-on projects in Big Data, Spark, and AWS.

Right now, I’m building something I’m really excited about — an AI-powered game at the AI Innovation Lab at Supercell. It blends real-time reasoning and world simulation to rethink how gameplay can feel truly dynamic and intelligent. It’s a space where my passion for AI and storytelling really comes alive.

Previously, I interned at Visa, where I worked on large-scale data pipelines in Apache Spark and Scala, tackling real-world data quality and infrastructure challenges. Before grad school, I completed my Bachelor’s in Information Technology at the College of Engineering, Guindy, diving deep into topics ranging from AI to OS, databases, and networks.

Outside the classroom and lab, I thrive in fast-paced, creative environments. I’m a frequent hackathon participant — I love the challenge of building something impactful, fast, and collaborative. It’s where many of my favorite ideas have started to take shape.
